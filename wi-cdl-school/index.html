You'll learn from the best.

When you train with the Wisconsin CDL School, you'll be among some of the safest, most effective and professional drivers in the nation. Our training curriculum focuses on driving, backing and developing the skills needed to safely operate a truck and trailer and prepares you to test for a Class A Commercial Driver's License (CDL)-the license you need to drive over-the-road semi-trucks.

Certified by the Wisconsin Educational Approval Board and the Wisconsin Department of Transportation (DOT), Wisconsin CDL School classes are taught by certified experienced driver instructors with a real-world focus-preparing graduates for truck driving jobs.

The CDL-A training program runs for three weeks. Truck driving school instructors are certified by the Wisconsin Educational Approval Board and have completed the Master Level of the Commercial Vehicle Training Association (CVTA) Instructor Certification Program. They have over 13.25 million miles over the road and 60 years of driving experience.

Get the skills you need.

You'll get the skills and training you need to be a safe, successful truck driver.

Week 1:

During your first week, you will learn how to safely operate a truck and trailer in a controlled, range environment and on public roadways, as well as complete a full pre-trip inspection.

Week 2:

During this week, you'll refine driving skills: shifting, turning, backing and learning to scan your environment, plus how to manage your vehicle in close-quarter areas.

Week 3:

During this week, you'll learn the other responsibilities of an over-the-road driver, such as federal, state and local regulations. Plus, you will learn atlas reading, hours of service rules and trip planning.
